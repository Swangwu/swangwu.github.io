Wind Turbine Power Curve Modelling Using Quantile Filtering and Neural Networks
Olayinka Ohunakin, Emerald Henry, Victor Ezekiel,
Code.

  1. We introduced a new method of obtaining a very accurate WTPC model.
  2. We considered several other variables including: density, wind speed, temperature, and blade pitch angle, to develop the WTPC model, other than wind speed only.
  3. We utilized three models namely: RBF, QF-MLP with 4-hidden layers, and QF-MLP with 6-hidden layers.
  4. We found that the QF-MLP with 6-hidden layers, gave the best performance and also the most sensitive model to dataset distribution
  5. The RBF network is the most stable network because it has the least standard deviation between the datasets.
  6. With this method, we can efficiently monitor wind turbines and wind farms, while detecting faults after every 120 minutes.
  7. We developed a method of fault detection based on the QF-NN algorithm to be implemented for real time monitoring of wind turbines in a wind farm.

(paper and will be updated before october)

Selected Projects


Quantile Filtering Algorithm

I developed a novel filteration algorithm that is used by 'the renewable energy research group' for the removal of outlier turbine data obtained via Supervisory control and data acquisition (SCADA) systems. It functions by defining quantiles on a normal destribution of the data.

Confidence Level Monitoring

Developed a statistical technique for detecting faulty wind turbines in wind farm monitoring. This method defines confidence levels on the two dimensional wind speed to power plot based on the euclidean distance between data points, and data point to the reference x-plane

Electro-pneumatic shifting system design

Designed an automatic gear shifting system that was implemented on Hebron Motorsports mini racecar. It functions by using a compressed air control loop to shift between gears.

Brake System Plausiblity Device PCB design

I was assisted by Bronco Racing Team in Designing and fabricating a 4 layer Printed circuit board that accepts signals from two analogue sensors and outputs a 5v signal for a certain duration of time if some thresholding conditions are exceeded.
