Experience

I am currently applying for Machine Learning and MLOps roles, and I am open to both relocation and remote work.

I previously worked at Clinton Health Access Initiative a public health organisation. I supported the Malaria, Essential Medicines and Oxygen programs with database development and management.This involved ensuring efficient data systems for these vital health initiatives. Before that, I served as a Machine Learning Researcher at the Molecular Biology Lab In Covenant University, During this time, my focus was on investigating various aspects of medical image analysis, including automatic detection, classification, segmentation, and registration of diverse medical images such as Whole Slide Images, MRI, and CT scans. My work aimed at advancing the understanding and application of machine learning techniques in the context of medical imaging for improved diagnostics and patient care. During my undergraduate, I was a Machine Learning student researcher at the Energy and Environment Research Group (TEERG). I was supervised by Professor Ohunakin Olayinka.

My interests revolve around harnessing machine learning to create products that address people's problems. I am particularly intrigued by solutions that lie at the intersection of natural language processing and graph neural networks, with a focus on knowledge graphs. Additionally, I am keen on exploring the realms of computer vision and databases.
